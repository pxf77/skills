# Fantasy Image-Drama Storyboard Framework

Use this reference to structure fantasy web-novel scenes as image-drama storyboards.

## Length Patterns

### 3-6 Frame Hook

Use for short concepts, cover groups, and opening hooks.

1. Establish conflict or humiliation.
2. Show protagonist reaction or hidden clue.
3. Escalate pressure.
4. Reveal power, identity, or artifact.
5. Show crowd or antagonist shock.
6. End on cliffhanger or dominant visual.

### 9-12 Frame Full Beat

Use for complete plot beats.

1. Establish location and status hierarchy.
2. Introduce protagonist under pressure.
3. Show antagonist action or accusation.
4. Show public reaction.
5. Insert hidden clue or emotional memory.
6. Trigger power, artifact, or identity reveal.
7. Expand magic effect or combat motion.
8. Reverse social pressure.
9. Show antagonist collapse or disbelief.
10. Add a cover-worthy wide shot.
11. Add a quiet emotional close-up.
12. End on next-scene cliffhanger.

## Shot Functions

- **Oppression:** high-angle shot, crowd pressure, protagonist isolated.
- **Setup:** wide scene, sect hall, palace steps, battlefield, secret realm.
- **Reaction:** close-up of eyes, clenched hand, broken token, trembling antagonist.
- **Ignition:** aura first appears, weapon wakes, runes glow, thunder gathers.
- **Manifestation:** full power display, spell formation, divine beast shadow, sky response.
- **Counterattack:** protagonist steps forward, enemy forced back, crowd silenced.
- **Reveal:** token, bloodline mark, divine seal, true robe, hidden title.
- **Aftermath:** stunned crowd, kneeling elders, cracked ground, fading magic.
- **Cliffhanger:** unopened gate, approaching army, second lightning strike, shadowed figure.

## Continuity Rules

Give every important character reusable anchors:

- Face and age impression
- Hair color and style
- Clothing silhouette and colors
- Weapon, artifact, jewelry, scars, or identity token
- Spiritual energy color and shape
- Emotional baseline

Repeat the anchors concisely in every OpenAI prompt. For example: "the young cultivator in a torn dark-blue robe, silver hairpin, and faint golden spiritual aura" should remain consistent across frames.

## Rhythm Rules

- Every 3-4 frames should contain a reveal, reversal, emotional escalation, or suspense point.
- Put the strongest visual beat near the final third of the sequence.
- Use a mix of wide shots, medium shots, close-ups, and reaction shots.
- Avoid making every frame a full-body power pose.
- Keep magic effects readable: one dominant aura color and one main visual symbol per character.
