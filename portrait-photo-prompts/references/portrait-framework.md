# Portrait Photography Framework

Use this reference to choose concrete photographic direction for portrait and photoshoot prompts.

## Creative Axes

- **Subject temperament:** calm, introspective, confident, cinematic, elegant, rebellious, fragile, mysterious, playful, composed, distant, warm, intense.
- **Pose and expression:** relaxed standing pose, seated three-quarter pose, slight turn toward camera, looking away, direct gaze, subtle smile, neutral expression, wind-swept movement, hand near face, leaning on a wall, walking through frame.
- **Wardrobe and styling:** tailored suit, silk dress, oversized coat, white shirt, knit sweater, leather jacket, minimalist jewelry, editorial makeup, clean natural makeup, wet-look hair, loose hair, sculptural styling.
- **Scene:** daylight apartment, quiet street at dusk, studio cyclorama, rooftop at night, hotel corridor, vintage room, lakeside, bookstore, subway platform, gallery, rain-soaked city street.
- **Lighting:** soft window light, golden-hour backlight, overcast diffused light, hard flash, Rembrandt lighting, butterfly lighting, neon rim light, low-key studio light, cinematic practical lights, softbox key light with gentle fill.
- **Lens and depth:** 35mm environmental portrait, 50mm natural perspective, 85mm classic portrait compression, 105mm close portrait, shallow depth of field, deep focus environmental context.
- **Composition:** close-up, half-body, full-body, centered composition, rule of thirds, negative space, layered foreground, frame within a frame, low angle, eye-level intimacy, cinematic wide crop.
- **Color and texture:** muted earth tones, cool blue shadows, warm amber highlights, black-and-white silver gelatin, pastel daylight, high-contrast editorial color, faded film grain, clean digital magazine finish.

## Common Directions

### Cinematic Portrait

Use a scene with narrative tension, practical light sources, dimensional shadows, restrained expression, and a film-still composition.

### Studio Fashion Editorial

Use controlled lighting, intentional wardrobe, precise posing, clean background, polished skin texture, and magazine-grade composition.

### Natural-Light Lifestyle

Use soft daylight, relaxed posture, lived-in environment, natural skin texture, understated wardrobe, and documentary realism.

### Vintage Film Portrait

Use analog grain, faded colors, gentle halation, imperfect texture, soft contrast, and a period-appropriate setting or styling.

### Dark Emotional Portrait

Use low-key lighting, negative space, deeper shadows, restrained colors, introspective expression, and minimal props.

### Fine-Art Portrait

Use painterly light, sculptural pose, refined wardrobe, controlled palette, symbolic props, and quiet formal composition.
